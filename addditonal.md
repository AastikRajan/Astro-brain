# Part A – Remedial Measures (Upāya) Rules

According to classical Jyotiṣa (BPHS Ch.84–86 and later commentaries), each planet has specific propitiatory measures.  In general, afflicted or weak planets are strengthened by wearing their gems, chanting their mantras, donating appropriate items on the planet’s weekday, using their yantra, and worshiping their presiding deity.  For example, Parāśara prescribes materials for idols: **Surya–copper, Chandra–rock crystal, Maṅgala–red sandalwood, Budha & Guru–gold, Śukra–silver, Śani–iron, Rahu–glass, Ketu–bell-metal**【8†L115-L124】.  He also gives mantra counts: chant **Sūrya’s mantra 7,000 times**, **Candra’s 11,000**, **Maṅgala’s 11,000**, **Budha/Guru 9,000**, **Śukra 16,000**, **Śani 23,000**, **Rāhu 8,000**, **Ketu 7,000**【8†L158-L164】.  Classical texts (BPHS) associate each planet with a gem, mantra, donation, yantra, and deity as follows:

- **Surya (Sun)**: Gem – Ruby (or red coral). Mantra – *“Om Ādityāya Namah”* (chant on Sunday). **Donation** – give wheat, jaggery or red cloth (often to Brahmins or public workers) on Sunday. **Yantra** – Surya Yantra (usually copper). **Deity** – Aditya (Sun god) or Lord Rāma (avatar of Surya). **Contra** – If Sūrya rules highly benefic houses or is exalted, one should not unduly weaken it by gemstone removal; also avoid if Sūrya is combust or retrograde.  

- **Chandra (Moon)**: Gem – Pearl (or white sapphire). Mantra – *“Om Somāyā Namah”* (chant on Monday). **Donation** – give rice, milk, white foods, or silver (especially to women or Brahmins) on Monday. **Yantra** – Chandra Yantra (silver or quartz). **Deity** – Śiva or Pārvatī (Moon is their son), or Shiva’s form Chandramukhi. **Contra** – Avoid if Chandra is lord of benefic houses in Kendra (like Taurus or Cancer lagna); do not wear Pearl if Chandra is debilitated (Scorpio) or in inimical sign.

- **Maṅgala (Mars)**: Gem – Red Coral. Mantra – *“Om Aṅgārakāya Namah”* (chant on Tuesday). **Donation** – give red lentils, meat (for Mahāhoma), or small red animals (as per folk tradition) on Tuesday. **Yantra** – Maṅgala Yantra or Hanuman Yantra (copper). **Deity** – Hanumān or Kārttikeya (Skanda). **Contra** – If Mars is lagnesha of an auspicious lagna or in own sign, do not unduly weaken it. Avoid Coral if Maṅgala is lord of 8th or 12th.

- **Budha (Mercury)**: Gem – Emerald. Mantra – *“Om Budhāya Namah”* (chant on Wednesday). **Donation** – give green foods or cloth on Wednesday. **Yantra** – Budha Yantra (gold). **Deity** – Kṛṣṇa or Viṣṇu (Budha’s deva). **Contra** – Do not wear if Mercury is lord of 2nd/11th (wealth), or debilitated.

- **Guru (Jupiter)**: Gem – Yellow Sapphire (Pukhraj). Mantra – *“Om Bṛhaspataye Namah”* (chant on Thursday). **Donation** – give yellow cloth, books, cow’s milk or gold (to Brahmins) on Thursday. **Yantra** – Guru Yantra (golden). **Deity** – Viṣṇu or Brihaspati. **Contra** – Avoid Pukhraj if Jupiter is exalted in Cancer and rules benefic houses; if Jupiter is combust or debilitated, use remedial mantras instead of stone.

- **Śukra (Venus)**: Gem – Diamond (or White Sapphire). Mantra – *“Om Śukrāya Namah”* (chant on Friday). **Donation** – give white garments, sweets, or white offerings (Friday). **Yantra** – Śukra Yantra (silver). **Deity** – Lakṣmī or Śukracharya. **Contra** – If Venus is extremely strong or lord of lagna, do not strengthen it to cause excess indulgence.

- **Śani (Saturn)**: Gem – Blue Sapphire (Neelam). Mantra – *“Om Śanaiścara Namah”* (chant on Saturday). **Donation** – give black sesame seeds, black cloth, or feed the poor/cows (Saturday). **Yantra** – Śani Yantra (iron). **Deity** – Śani Dev or Hanumān. **Contra** – If Saturn rules important benefic houses (like 9th for an Aries lagna), avoid weakening it. Never wear Neelam if you are not expert or if Saturn is ardra or Jyeshṭha nakṣatra lord (extreme malefic).

- **Rāhu**: Gem – Hessonite (Gomed). Mantra – *“Om Rāmaggha Namah”* or *“Om Raṁ Raḥuḥ”* (chant on Wednesday). **Donation** – give dark items (black blankets, blue flowers) especially to Nāgas or Brahmins on Rāhu’s weekday (Wednesday/Thursday evening). **Yantra** – Rahu Yantra (black glass). **Deity** – Ganeśa or Śiva (to pacify Rāhu). **Contra** – If Rāhu is lord of a benefic house (e.g. 5th/9th), avoid weakening it; do not wear Gomed if Rāhu is exalted.

- **Ketu**: Gem – Cat’s Eye (Lehsunia). Mantra – *“Om Keṁ Ketave Namah”* (chant on Thursday). **Donation** – give mustard seeds, blankets, camphor in Ketu-ruled temples on Thursday. **Yantra** – Ketu Yantra (bell-metal). **Deity** – Bhairava or Navagraha image. **Contra** – If Ketu is a natural benefic in your chart, avoid weakening it; do not wear Cat’s Eye if Ketu is in own sign.

*(Sources: Parāśara’s BPHS ch.86 (via modern commentary) lists the sacred metals for each planet【8†L115-L124】 and prescribed mantra counts【8†L158-L164】.  Additional rules come from classical muhurta texts and Astrological tradition.)*  

# Part B – Muhūrta (Electional Astrology) Rules

**Workflow**: For any event type, filter date-times by Panchāṅga criteria and event-specific rules:

1. **Remove globally inauspicious times**.  Exclude **eclipses (solar/lunar)** and any **Mahā Sankranti** (Sun’s ingress), as these are universally bad for sacred events【44†L428-L432】.  Avoid **amānta new-moon (Amāvāsya)** and **pūrṇimā (Full Moon)** tithis for major ceremonies.  Also exclude certain lower tithis (Chaturthī–4th, Aṣṭamī–8th, Navamī–9th) which classical texts counsel against for auspicious rites.  

2. **Filter by week-day**.  Marriage muhurta typically avoids **Tuesday, Saturday, and eclipse days**.  Business/electional events often avoid **Saturday**.  Confirm per tradition.

3. **Filter by nakṣatra and yog**.  For marriage, choose auspicious stars (e.g. **Rohiṇī, Mrigashīrṣā, Uttara Phālguni, Uttara Ashādhā, Hasta, Śvatī** etc.) and avoid known bad stars (e.g. **Aśvinī, Bharanī, Aṣleṣā, Jyeṣṭhā, Śatabhishak, Mūla, Revati**).  (Sources: classical muhurta texts and modern experts list 12 nakṣatras “incompatible” for marriage.)  For business or other events, analogous good/bad nakṣatras apply (use green/red lists from pan̄chāṅga tradition).

4. **Basic Panchāṅga checks**.  Compute tithi, vār (weekday), nakṣatra, yoga, karaṇa at each candidate time.  For marriage, ensure the tithi and nakṣatra are both auspicious.  For naming ceremony (nāmakaraṇa), ensure the letter sound matches the bride’s and groom’s rāśī [customary rule].  

5. **Varga and house checks**.  For wedding: ensure the rāśī lagna or mūhurta lagna does not fall in 6th/8th/12th houses from natal or spouse’s lagna (avoid vāra and maraka).  For business: avoid lagna in last pada of nakṣatra (maraka).  Check kuṇḍalī and transits: e.g. avoid Jyeṣṭhā lagna or Mūla lagna for marriages.  

6. **Rank and select windows**.  After filtering, rate windows by auspicious yogas, benefic aspects, etc.  For weddings: best is śubh āmāvasya mārga and śrī yoga, also Lagna with benefics.  Output top-scoring 1–3 time spans.

**Event-specific highlights**:  
- **Wedding**: Avoid the listed tithis above.  Avoid Mangala (Tuesday) and Śāni (Saturday) for Vivāha Lagna.  Avoid Rāśi Lagna in AR (libra), or Sun in Gemini, Ketu in 8th, etc (per practitioner rules).  Auspicious: Rohiṇī, Bharaṇī, Mṛgaśīrṣā, Uttara Phālguni, Hasta, Śvāti; avoid Nāṭṭuṇa, Śata-bhiṣaj, Dhanishta (examples from classical lists).  Also align bride/groom rāśī nakṣatra groups (chatuṣpāda groups) for compatibility. *(See traditional vivaha muhurta guides.)*  

- **Business start**: Similar Panchāṅga screens; prefer Friday or Monday Lagna with benefics.  Choose Lagna in vargottama (especially Sun/Moon strong) for career success.

- **Medical procedure**: Avoid Dashami (10th), Chaturdashi (14th), and nakṣatras like Aṣleṣā, Jyeṣṭhā, Pūrvā Phālguni.  Avoid Rahu/Gulika kāla.  Prefer days ruled by benefics (e.g. Mercury lagna on Wednesday).

- **Property purchase**: Use vacant 4th-house lagna (earth sign) times.  Avoid maraka lagnas.  Good: Mercury or Venus lagna in dwelling signs (Taurus, Cancer, Libra, Capricorn).  Avoid ekādaśī, dvitīyā tithi.

- **Naming ceremony**: Tithi must match the puppy (nakṣatra pada) of birth star.  Avoid katuṇḍinī and viśālā yogas.  

*In all cases, first apply minimal checks (forbidden tithi/weekday/varā) to narrow candidates, then full Panchāṅga evaluation (tithi, nakṣatra, yoga, karaṇa, lagna, etc) to rank the final muhurta windows.*  

*(Sources: Classical muhurta treatises (e.g. Muhurta Chintāmaṇi) and contemporary panchāṅga guidelines.  E.g. Srīmad Bhāgavatam and Kalāprakāśikā caution against eclipses for ceremonies【44†L428-L432】.)*  

# Part C – Divisional Chart (Varga) Rules

**D9 (Navāṃśa)** – *Marriage and inner maturity chart.*  A planet is **vargottama** if it is in the same rāśī (sign) in D1 and D9.  Vargottama planets retain strength and give steady results【15†L165-L168】.  (Practically, in a movable rāśi this means the planet lies in the **first 3°20′**; in a fixed rāśi, in the **last 3°20′**; in a dual rāśi, the **last 3°20′** – see standard nakṣatra-pāda mapping rules.)  A vargottama planet is as strong as possible across divisional layers.

A **Pushkara Navāṃśa** is a special auspicious navāṃśa.  There are 24 Pushkara points (two in each sign) associated with benefics (Moon, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus).  For example, Aries’ Pushkara Navāṃśas are **Bharani pāda3 and Kṛttikā pāda1**【53†L90-L98】 (roughly 20°–23°20′ and 26°40′–30° Aries).  Kumāran (2023) tabulates all 24 Pushkara positions【53†L90-L98】.  A planet in a Pushkara navāṃśa gains special strength in that sign.  

*D9 Interpretation*:  D9 “matures” the D1 chart.  Planets in benefic signs in D9 support positive marriage/inner growth; debilitated or malefic placements indicate challenges.  Vargottama planets yield consistent results【15†L165-L168】.  In predictions, both the D1 and D9 placement of a planet are considered: e.g. a planet debilitated in D1 may not give its full malefic effect if exalted or in vargottama navāṃśa in D9, and vice versa.

**D10 (Daśāṃśa)** – *Career/profession chart.*  The 10th house of D10 reflects profession and reputation.  Key rules【18†L47-L55】【18†L57-L60】:
- If **D10 Lagna lord** is strong (own sign/exalted), career is stable【18†L47-L54】.
- **Sun, Saturn or Mercury** in their own sign or Kendra (1,4,7,10) of D10 gives good profession【18†L47-L54】.
- **Sun** (Śaṅkara) strong/exalted => leadership; **Saturn** in Upachaya (3,6,10,11) => steady success【18†L47-L54】.
- **9th house lord in D10’s 9th** – fortune/mentors in career【18†L51-L55】.
- **Debilitated/Dark Lagna lord or 6th/9th lord in D10** – career setbacks【18†L57-L60】.
- Any **planets in 6th/8th/12th from D10 Lagna** bring obstacles to profession.

To get D10 Lagna: divide each Rāśi into 10 parts (3° each); assign signs starting from D1 Lagna by counting in the sequence of movable/fixed/dual as in D9 but with 10 divisions. (In practice one can use standard D10 calculators.)

**D7 (Saptāṃśa)** – *Children chart.*  Key factors:
- **D7 5th house** is primary for fertility/children: benefics here indicate easy conception and healthy children; malefics indicate delays/problems【30†L251-L259】.
- **Number of children**: traditionally gauged by **number of planets in D7’s 5th house** (or aspected by the D7 5th lord), excluding debilitated/combust planets【33†L249-L252】.  
- **Other houses**: D7 **1st house** shows overall fertility constitution; benefics here favor progeny, malefics (especially Saturn) indicate delays【30†L244-L253】.  D7 **7th house** (spouse influence) – afflictions here mean spouse carries fertility karma【30†L258-L263】.  D7 **9th house** – signifies grandchildren and lineage【30†L264-L273】.  D7 **2nd house** – resources for child-rearing【30†L270-L279】.  D7 **11th house** – gains from children/support in old age【30†L276-L279】.
  
*(Sources: Classical texts (Jātaka Parijāta, Uttara-kālmṛta) stress the D7’s 5th house and Jupiter for progeny.  Dr. Meenakshi Sharma notes: “Benefics in D7’s 5th promise easy conception; malefics indicate challenges”【30†L251-L259】.  AstroShastra also states the number of planets in D7’s 5th (or linked to its lord) equals number of children【33†L249-L252】.)*

**D4 (Chaturthāṃśa)** – *Property/comfort chart.*  This chart “primarily focuses on happiness, comforts, and properties, including immovable assets like land and buildings”【28†L62-L70】.  Houses of D4:
- D4 **1st house** – overall wealth and happiness;
- D4 **4th house** – actual property/real estate (lords and planets here indicate assets);
- D4 **9th house** – inherited wealth/patrimonial luck;
- D4 **11th house** – gains from assets (rental income, profits)【34†L1-L4】.
Planets here: Benefics in D4-4th and D4-Lagna suggest property acquisition; malefics (or Saturn) suggest property delays.  **Property timing**: favorable mahā-dāśā of planets strong in D4 (or transits to D4-4th) often coincide with acquiring land.  *(Sources: MysticGazer notes D4’s focus on property【28†L62-L70】.)*

**D60 (Shashtyāṃśa)** – *Karmic/subtle chart.*  Parāśara names each 1/60th portion after deities【26†L108-L117】.  In an odd rāśi, the Shashtyāṃśa sequence is: 1-Ghora (krūra), 2-Rākṣasa (krūra), 3-Deva (śubha), 4-Kubera (śubha), 5-Yakṣa (śubha), … up to 60 (Chandra-rekha)【26†L108-L117】.  Mantreśvara (Phaladeepika) lists which 1–60 are auspicious: in odd signs the **kroora (inauspicious)** 1/60ths are numbers **1,2,8–12,15–16,30–35,39–44,48,51–52,59**【26†L174-L179】 (all others are śubha).  In even signs, this is reversed.  A planet in a śubha Shashtyāṃśa bestows good fortune; in a kroora one, it gives difficulties【26†L171-L179】.  Thus D60 fine-tunes results: even a benefic planet can give inauspicious effects if lodged in a kroora shashtyāṃśa, and vice versa.  

*(Sources: BPHS 6.33–41 gives the 60 names【26†L108-L117】.  Phaladeepika (via Mantresvara) assigns śubha/kroora status to each 1/60【26†L174-L179】.)*

**Summary**:  Divisional charts are used thematically.  D9 refines marriage and inner strength (vargottama→consistency【15†L165-L168】, Pushkara→extra auspicious).  D10 refines career (D10 Lagna/10th house and strong Karakas＝success【18†L47-L54】【18†L57-L60】).  D7 refines progeny (5th house planets and Jupiter being primary indicators【30†L251-L259】【33†L249-L252】).  D4 refines property (4th-house assets, 9th inheritance【28†L62-L70】).  D60 refines all results to microscopic detail via auspicious (śubha) vs inauspicious (krūra) portions【26†L108-L117】【26†L174-L179】.

**Sources**: Classic Jyotiṣa texts (BPHS, Jātaka Parijāta, Uttara-kālmṛta, Phaladeepika), and modern interpretations.  Citations above indicate specific traditional rules.
