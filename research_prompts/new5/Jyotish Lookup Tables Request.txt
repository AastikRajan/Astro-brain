Classical Jyotish Computational Matrices: Algorithms, Arrays, and Astrological Mechanics
The translation of classical Vedic astrology (Jyotish) into a programmatic environment requires the strict codification of ancient Sanskrit dictums into structured, multidimensional arrays. In classical computational astrology, determinism is modeled through rigid algorithmic rulesets that quantify relational compatibility, karmic potential, and temporal event triggers. The following analysis exhaustively details the core lookup tables required to build a functional astrological engine. The arrays provided herein are exact, unabridged, and formatted for direct translation into application logic, avoiding arbitrary summaries to preserve the computational integrity of the system.
Alongside the structural data, a rigorous analysis of the underlying astrological mechanics is provided, delineating the causal relationships, geometric overrides, and astronomical logic that dictate how these variables interact within a broader deterministic framework.
1. Yoni Koota: The Biological and Instinctual Resonance Array
Yoni Koota operates as a biological compatibility algorithm within the Ashtakoota marriage matching system. Assigned a weight of 4 out of 36 points (11%), it quantifies physical harmony, sexual compatibility, and subconscious instinctual bonding.1 The algorithm groups the 27 lunar mansions (Nakshatras) into 14 distinct animal archetypes, each polarized with a specific gender representation.2
The scoring logic functions on a rigid 0 to 4 scalar array. Identical animal pairings yield maximum physical resonance (4 points, indicating 91% satisfaction). Pairs considered naturally friendly score 3 points (78%), neutral pairs generate 2 points (65%), and unfriendly pairs yield 1 point (48%).1 Third-order analysis reveals that the 0-point pairs (e.g., Cat and Rat, Serpent and Mongoose) do not merely represent lack of attraction; they trigger a "Yoni Dosha," representing deeply ingrained predator-prey dynamics or severe territorial conflicts that fundamentally destabilize the energetic union, yielding a basal 28-38% satisfaction rate.1

	3. Bhakoot Koota: Lunar Geometry and Prosperity Algorithm
Bhakoot (Rasi Koota) assesses the geometric and spatial relationship between the natal Moon signs of the prospective couple. Securing a pivotal 7 points out of the 36-point system, it correlates heavily with long-term financial stability, physical health, and relational longevity.9 The algorithm categorizes certain spatial relationships (1/1, 1/7, 3/11, 4/10) as auspicious, granting 7 points.9 Conversely, angular distances of 2/12, 5/9, and 6/8 trigger "Bhakoot Dosha," yielding 0 points.9
The 6/8 axis generates the most profound instability; one partner's moon sits in the 8th house (transformation, sudden crisis) of the other, establishing a vector of continuous energetic drain and financial turbulence.10

	4. The Shashtiamsha (D60) Karmic Granularity Array
The Shashtiamsha (D60) chart represents the highest resolution divisional architecture in standard Jyotish. Each 30-degree zodiac sign is partitioned into 60 discrete segments of 30 arc-minutes (0.5 degrees).14 Because the Ascendant of the D60 chart shifts approximately every 2 minutes of clock time, this array maps past-life Sanchita Karma—the deeply aggregated causal seeds from previous incarnations—bypassing superficial psychological markers entirely.14
Sage Parashara establishes the absolute supremacy of the D60, assigning it the highest Vimshopaka Bala weight (4 points out of 20) in the Shadvarga scheme.15 The programmatic generation of the D60 requires bifurcated logic: odd signs (Aries, Gemini, Leo, Libra, Sagittarius, Aquarius) sequence the deities in direct order (1 to 60), while even signs (Taurus, Cancer, Virgo, Scorpio, Capricorn, Pisces) sequence them in strict reverse order (60 down to 1).15 Furthermore, segments categorized as "Krura" (malefic/inauspicious) in odd signs invert their polarity to become "Soumya" (benefic/auspicious) in even signs.15

	6. The 16 Tajika Yogas: The Relational Calculus of Varshaphala
The Tajika system, employed primarily in Varshaphala (annual horoscopy) and Prashna (horary astrology), abandons the standard Parashari sign-based aspects in favor of 16 highly specific geometric operators known as "Yogas".19 These relational operators calculate the exact timing, energetic flow, and manifestation of physical events.
Computational analysis of Tajika Yogas relies on rigid Deeptamshas (orbs of influence) for each planet: Sun 15°, Moon 12°, Mars 8°, Mercury 7°, Jupiter 9°, Venus 7°, and Saturn 9°.19 The algorithm must also strictly monitor planetary velocity, following the hierarchy from fastest to slowest: Moon, Mercury, Venus, Sun, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn.19 An aspect only mathematically completes if the faster planet applies to the slower planet within the designated orb.
