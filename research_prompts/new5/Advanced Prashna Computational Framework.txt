Computational Frameworks for Advanced Prashna Shastra: Algorithmic Systems and Divinatory Logic
The mathematical and symbolic architecture of Prashna Shastra, or horary astrology, represents one of the most sophisticated branches of the Jyotish tradition. Unlike natal astrology, which relies upon a static moment of birth, Prashna treats the moment of a sincere inquiry as a biological birth of a specific karma, manifesting through the "Prashna Shakti" or the power of the question itself.1 The techniques originating from the Kerala tradition, specifically those detailed in the seminal text Prashna Marga, utilize a multi-layered computational framework that integrates astronomical longitudes, numerological reductions of physical objects, phonetic mapping of human speech, and the systematic observation of environmental omens.3
Ashtamangala Prashna: The Full Computational System
Ashtamangala Prashna is regarded as the most comprehensive diagnostic tool in the horary tradition, typically reserved for profound systemic inquiries such as temple sanctity, ancestral curses, and life-altering familial blockages.6 The term "Ashtamangalam" refers to eight auspicious materials—traditionally including gold, a mirror, flowers, consecrated rice, fruits, betel leaves, sacred texts, and a ghee lamp—which serve to bridge the gap between the mundane and the cosmic.6
The 108-Cell Board and House Mapping
The foundational grid for this technique is the Ashtottaramsa or the 108-cell board. This layout is not merely a symbolic representation but a functional mapping of the D-108 divisional chart.9 The grid is constructed by dividing each of the twelve zodiac signs into nine subdivisions (Navamsas), creating a total of 108 cells (  ).
The mapping of these cells is structured to analyze the triadic flow of karma. The board represents the totality of a soul's journey, with specific cell ranges corresponding to different lifetimes. The first through fourth houses (cells 1–36) represent the past life and ancestral roots.9 The fifth through eighth houses (cells 37–72) represent the present incarnation and current struggles.9 The ninth through twelfth houses (cells 73–108) represent the future life and the ultimate potential for spiritual liberation.9

The Rashiprashna Variant
Within the larger Ashtamangala system, the Rashiprashna (or sign-based inquiry) focuses specifically on the zodiacal signs activated during the ritual. This involves the Swarna Lagna (Gold Lagna), where a piece of gold is placed within the Rasi Chakra.7 The astrologer observes not just the sign occupied by the gold, but also the orientation of the coin. If the coin is face down, tilted toward the South, or slanted, it is interpreted as a severe omen of death or decline (Arishta), regardless of the mathematical strength of the chart.8

Aksharachakra: The Phonetic Geometry of Prashna
Aksharachakra, or phonetic astrology, operates on the principle that the sounds uttered at the moment of inquiry are non-random vibrational signatures of the cosmic state. By mapping the first syllable (Akshara) of a question to a specific sign, the astrologer determines the Arudha (the activated sign) even without a traditional time-based chart.12

	Determining the Arudha via Speech
The process of using Aksharachakra requires the astrologer to isolate the very first sound that initiates the communication. For example, if a querent asks, "Is my health improving?", the first syllable is "I" (short vowel). According to the lookup table, this sound is a Svara, activating Aries (Mesha) as the Arudha Lagna.14
This phonetic Arudha is then compared to the Udaya Lagna (the rising sign based on the time of the question). If the phonetic Arudha and the time-based Lagna are in harmony (e.g., trinal or quadrangular relationship), the question is deemed sincere and the outcome likely favorable.2 If the phonemes belong to a category ruled by a malefic planet that is poorly placed in the Prashna chart, it indicates that the querent may be hiding information or that the question itself is "mute" (mooka).18
Nimitta Shastra: Environmental Synchronization and Omen Classification
Nimitta Shastra is the science of interpreting spontaneous environmental cues. In the Prashna Marga, the environment is seen as a mirror of the querent's internal reality. These omens provide a validation layer that can override purely mathematical calculations.11

	Systematic Classification and Decision Tree Logic
Omens are classified into three primary categories, allowing them to be coded into a systematic decision tree for rapid diagnosis during a session.11
1. Sharirika (Bodily) Omens:
      * Twitching: If a female's left eye twitches, it is auspicious (success); if the right eye twitches, it is inauspicious (delay/failure).11
      * Sneezing: A single sneeze before starting a task is a warning to delay; two sneezes are considered a clearing of the path.22
      * Itching: Itching of the right hand signifies incoming wealth; left hand signifies expenses.22
2. Drishya (Visual/Object) Omens:
      * Broken Glass: Contrary to Western superstition, the sudden breaking of glass is often seen as the "breaking of the evil eye" (nazar), indicating a release from a negative cycle.22
      * Ghee Lamp: If the flame leans toward the South, it indicates destruction or "yamakantaka" influence; a steady flame signifies stability.8
      * Flowers/Fruits: Seeing blooming plants at the time of inquiry signifies Jupiterian protection and growth.3
3. Decision Tree for Outcome Prediction:
      * Step 1: Is the omen Sharirika, Drishya, or Shakuna?
      * Step 2: Does it involve a planet of the "Harm House" (Badha)?
      * Step 3: If multiple omens point toward the same result (e.g., a crow on the right shoulder AND a right-hand itch), the prediction is certain.11

Advanced Prashna Timing: Voice Signs and Multipliers
The chronometry of Prashna differs from natal astrology in its use of "dynamic multipliers." Timing is determined by the relationship between the rising sign (Lagna), the planet ruling the Navamsa, and the specific "vocal" quality of the signs.18
Mooka (Mute) vs. Speaking Signs
The classification of signs as "Mute" (Mooka) or "Speaking" determines the speed and clarity with which an event will manifest.18
Speaking (Voice/Musical) Signs:
      * Signs: Gemini, Libra, Aquarius.18
      * Secondary Signs: Virgo, Sagittarius.18
      * Indication: Rapid manifestation. Information will be shared openly. Legal outcomes will be vocalized quickly. Success comes through negotiation.18
Mooka (Mute) Signs:
      * Signs: Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces.18
      * Indication: Delays. Hidden or "silent" agendas. Information is withheld. Events manifest slowly and without public recognition. If the 2nd lord is in a mute sign and afflicted, the querent is likely lying or unable to express the true nature of their problem.18

The direction of the sign—whether it is rising (ascending toward the MC) or setting (descending toward the IC)—further modifies the timing. A planet in a "Rising" sign indicates an event approaching the querent, while a "Setting" sign indicates an opportunity that has already passed (Separating Aspect).5
Trisphutam: The Mathematical Index of Vitality and Validation
Trisphutam, or the "Three-fold Longitude," is one of the most critical calculations for validating the health of the querent and the authenticity of the chart. It acts as a composite indicator of the physical (Lagna), mental (Moon), and karmic/poisonous (Gulika) states.25
The Computational Formula
The Trisphutam is the sum of the longitudes of the Lagna, the Moon, and Gulika (the malefic sub-planet associated with Saturn).25
  

If the sum exceeds 360 degrees, 360 is subtracted to find the point within the zodiac. This point is then mapped to its Rasi, Navamsa, and Nakshatra for analysis.25

Validation of the Chart
The Trisphutam is used to check the "honesty" of the inquiry. If the Trisphutam is heavily afflicted by "Nava Doshas" (nine specific planetary blemishes) or if Gulika is conjunct the Trisphuta Rasi, the entire chart is considered "poisoned." In such cases, the predictions will not manifest as expected because the querent's negative karma or lack of sincerity is clouding the divine response.25
Synthesis of Computational Frameworks
The integration of these six systems—Ashtamangala, Aksharachakra, Nimitta, Shakuna, Advanced Timing, and Trisphutam—creates a comprehensive divinatory engine. The Ashtamangala provides the Broad Karmic Vector (Past/Present/Future), the Aksharachakra provides the Linguistic Verification, the Nimitta/Shakuna provide Environmental Feedback, and the Trisphutam provides the Biological/Vitality Baseline.7
By following these algorithms, the practitioner can move from a simple horary chart to a multi-dimensional diagnostic report that accounts for the querent's speech, physical presence, environment, and deepest karmic history. The use of modulo arithmetic, phonetic mapping, and zonal longitude analysis ensures that the prediction is not a matter of subjective intuition but a rigorous mathematical derivation.
