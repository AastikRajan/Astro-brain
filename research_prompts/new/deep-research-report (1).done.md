# Graha Combustion (Asta/Moudya) Rules

- **Combustion orbs:** Classical texts (e.g. *Sūrya Siddhānta* as cited by Visti Larsen) give the following Sun-combustion orbs: Mars 17°, Saturn 15°, Jupiter 11°, Venus 10° (8° when retrograde), Mercury 14° (12° retrograde)【30†L307-L314】.  (Some modern tables also list Moon ~12°, though Moon is usually exempt as a luminary.)  Within ~3°20′ of the Sun a planet is fully “burnt up” (astāṅgata), beyond that up to the above limits it is partially combust (mōdhya or “dull”)【25†L205-L210】. 

- **Retrograde exception:**  Classical sources do *not* nullify combustion for retrograde planets – instead they give smaller orbs.  Thus Mercury’s orb is 14° direct, 12° retro; Venus 10° direct, 8° retro【30†L307-L314】.  (A modern commentator notes Mercury retrograde “gains energy” and is often treated as non-combust【44†L252-L260】, but Surya Siddhanta explicitly lists a 12° orb for Mercury retrograde【30†L307-L314】.) 

- **Graha Yuddha + combustion:** Classical texts treat planetary war (yuddha) and combustion as separate afflictions.  The afflicted planet loses strength from both.  In effect both conditions “stack.”  (For example, leading astrologers note effects are *additive*, not canceling each other【23†L64-L70】.)  Thus a planet defeated in war and also combust is doubly weak.  (No classic verse gives a combined formula; one applies both sets of rules.)

- **Effect on Śaṣṭiāṃśa (Ṣaḍbala):** Combustion is essentially a state of extreme weakness.  In classics a combust planet “loses its own potency”【44†L275-L284】.  No classical text gives a simple Shashtiamsa subtraction, but Uttara Kalamrita explicitly states **“benefic planets if combust lose their potency, while malefics become more mischievous”**【25†L159-L163】.  In practice one treats a combust planet’s strength as almost nullified (e.g. zero Chesta-bala, very low Dig-bala). 

- **Exaltation vs. combustion:** Both conditions co-exist.  Exaltation is not cancelled by combustion; rather each contributes.  E.g. BPHS (Labha-house stanza) says: *“If the lord of the 11th is exalted, though in combustion there will still be many gains.”*【25†L222-L230】.  In other words, an exalted combust planet still gives positive results (albeit delayed), and an exalted state mitigates some harm.  (Astrologers note “effects are additive”【23†L64-L70】 rather than one nullifying the other.)

- **Moon near Sun (New Moon):**  Moon’s close approach to the Sun is simply the dark moon (amāvasya) phase, not classically treated as “graha combustion.”  Classical texts do not list Moon in their combustion orbs (and often say only Mercury–Saturn burn).  In shadbala/dosha, a near-Sun Moon is considered just weak (Chandra-Adhana), not a separate combustion category.

- **Combustion during *Daśā*:**  If an active daśā- or antardaśā-lord is combust in the natal chart, its period is weakened.  For example, Brihat Parāśara (47.49–51) says natal Jupiter combust in Jupiter-daśā gives poor results early and only later honors【53†L121-L129】.  Similarly, Saturn/Venus combust do not shorten longevity but make the daśā “troublesome”【53†L125-L129】.  (No classical source gives an exact % reduction; empirically one treats a combust daśā-lord as only partially effective.)  

- **Combustion in transit:**  A planet becoming combust during transit is considered ineffective.  Classical advice (e.g. *Sarvartha Chintamani*) is that planets in *āgocharāvasthā* (debilitation or combustion) during gochara yield unfavorable results【57†L43-L45】.  In practice one discounts or severely downplays the transit of a combusted planet. 

- **Nodes (Rāhu/Ketu):**  Rāhu and Ketu have no combustion.  They are mathematical points and not luminous, so *graha-mōdyatva* does not apply【25†L150-L154】.  (Some modern writers mention a “mēshaṇyōga” when Rahu is ~9° from the Sun, but this has no basis in classical rules.)

- **Vedha (Obstruction):**  If a planet is transiting a favourable house, any planet occupying its listed *Vedha houses* blocks or cancels its good effects【68†L205-L213】【68†L214-L220】. Likewise, Moon’s benefic houses (1,3,6,7,10,11) have Vedha from 2,4,5,8,9【68†L210-L213】, Mars’s from 1,2,5,9【68†L214-L217】, etc., as shown above.

# Additional Notes

- **Lagna vs. Moon:**  Classical gochara rules use the Moon’s chart as reference (Chandra-rāśi).  The above matrix is universal (not depending on one’s Ascendant).  There is no separate classical “Ascendant gochara” table in the traditional canon (some modern astrologers analogize by symmetry, but Parāśara and others emphasize Moon-based transit houses【59†L323-L331】).

- **Saturn in Ashtama (8th from Moon):**  Saturn in the 8th house from the Moon is extremely inauspicious (*Ashtama Śāni*).  It intensifies misfortune (sudden losses, chronic disease, delays or death) far more than Saturn in other houses.  This transit lasts ~2½ years (Saturn’s one-sign transit), during which time care is advised.

- **Jupiter’s auspicious transits:**  Jupiter transiting 2nd, 5th, 7th, 9th or 11th from the Moon is very beneficial【65†L170-L172】.  Classically:  2nd – wealth/family gains; 5th – success in creativity, children and speculations; 7th – prosperous partnership or marriage; 9th – fortune, auspicious long journeys or higher learning; 11th – gains, fulfillment of desires.  In each case Jupiter greatly amplifies the house significations (e.g. “gains, comforts, travel”【65†L170-L172】).
