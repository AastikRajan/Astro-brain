# Ashtottari Dasha (108-year cycle)

**Eligibility:**  Ashtottari is a *conditional* Nakshatra dasha invoked only when Rahu plays a special role.  Parashara states that Ashtottari is adopted **“when Rahu not being in Lagna, in any other Kendra or Trikona to the Lord of the Lagna”**【5†L5481-L5483】.  In practice this means Rahu must *not* be in the Ascendant itself, but must occupy an angular (1st/4th/7th/10th) or trinal (1st/5th/9th) house relative to the Ascendant‐lord’s position【5†L5481-L5483】.  (If this Rahu condition holds, astrologers use Ashtottari alongside or instead of Vimshottari; otherwise Vimshottari alone is used.) 

**Dasha Lords & Durations:**  Ashtottari uses 8 grahas (Ketu is omitted) with the following *year-lengths* for their Mahādashas【5†L5491-L5492】【20†L405-L412】:  

- **Sun:** 6 years  
- **Moon:** 15 years  
- **Mars:** 8 years  
- **Mercury:** 17 years  
- **Saturn:** 10 years  
- **Jupiter:** 19 years  
- **Rahu:** 12 years  
- **Venus:** 21 years  

(The total is 108 years.)  These exactly mirror Parashara’s list【5†L5491-L5492】.  In calculation, the first Mahadasha (starting lord) is determined by the Moon’s *Janma Nakshatra* – just as in Vimshottari – but using the Ashtottari nakshatra-to-planet mapping below.

**Nakshatra → Dasha Lord Mapping:**  In Ashtottari the 27 lunar mansions are grouped (Ketu omitted) as follows【20†L393-L401】:  

- **Sun** rules 4 nakshatras starting from **Ardra** (Nakshatras 6–9: Ardra, Punarvasu, Pushya, Ashlesha).  
- **Moon** rules the next 3 (10–12: Magha, Purva Phalguni, Uttara Phalguni).  
- **Mars** rules the next 4 (13–16: Hasta, Chitra, Swati, Vishakha).  
- **Mercury** rules the next 3 (17–19: Anuradha, Jyeshtha, Mula).  
- **Saturn** rules the next 3 (20–22: Purvashadha, Uttarashadha, Shravana).  
- **Jupiter** rules the next 3 (23–25: Dhanishta, Shatabhisha, Purvashadha*). *(Here “Purvashadha” as #25 means continuing past 27 back to 1, see below.)*  
- **Rahu** rules the next 4 nakshatras: (26–27 then 1–2: Dhanishta, Shatabhisha, **Ashwini**, **Bharani**).  
- **Venus** rules the final 3 (3–5: Krittika, Rohini, Mrigashira).  

Thus the count loops after 27 back to Ashwini(1), Bharani(2).  The Mahadasha at birth is the planet ruling the natal Moon’s nakshatra, and its *balance* is proportional to how far Moon has traversed in that nakshatra.  (This is calculated exactly like Vimshottari: multiply the planet’s full Dasha years by the fraction of the nakshatra already elapsed, subtract from the full years to get the remaining years.)

**Balance at Birth (Surplus Dasha):**  As in Vimshottari, the remaining portion of the Mahadasha at birth equals the fraction of the natal Moon‐nakshatra still to go.  Parashara’s method is identical: multiply the planet’s full Dasha length by the elapsed degrees of Moon in the Janma Nakshatra and divide by the Nakshatra’s total span【30†L220-L228】.  The remainder of the Dasha is then (full years – expired years).  (In Ashtottari, one subtlety Parashara notes: malefic rulers count 1 nakshatra as ¼ of their Dasha and benefics 1 nakshatra as ⅓, but the net effect is that the **same formula** as Vimshottari applies【30†L220-L228】.)  

**Sub-periods (Antar, Pratyantar, etc.):**  Sub-periods in Ashtottari follow exactly the same proportional scheme as Vimshottari.  That is, within each Mahadasha the sequence of Antar (biography) dashas proceeds in the fixed order of planets (Sun→Moon→…→Venus) and their durations are scaled in proportion.  Typically one computes each Antar Dasha by:  

> *Antar of planet X within Mahadasha of planet M = (length of the **remaining** M-Mahadasha) × (X’s full Dasha years) ÷ 120.*  

Since Ashtottari’s total cycle is 108 (not 120), some modern astrologers use 108 instead of 120 in these formulas, but the classical texts imply “the same method”【30†L220-L228】, so the **proportion** is identical.  (Similarly, Pratyantar, Sukshma, Prana, etc. are each obtained by multiplying the span of the parent sub-period by the sub-period lord’s Mahadasha years and dividing by 120.) 

**Interpretation & Cross-verification:**  Parashara does not enumerate special predictive themes for Ashtottari; he primarily gives calculation rules.  In practice, astrologers consider Ashtottari relevant to Rahu-strong charts and use it in conjunction with Vimshottari.  For example, if both Vimshottari and Ashtottari Mahadashas coincide (same planet), that period is treated as doubly significant.  Some modern sources suggest Ashtottari can accentuate sudden, unusual or long-distance (Rahu-like) events or spiritual transformations, but there is no explicit classical pronouncement on which life areas it “describes better.”  Generally one uses Ashtottari **in addition to** Vimshottari for greater resolution: if both systems point to the same timing, the prediction is strengthened (if they differ, the chart’s Rahu rule usually indicates which system is primary)【5†L5481-L5483】【30†L220-L228】.  

# Vimshottari Dasha Subdivisions (Levels 4–6)

Vimshottari’s subsidiary dashas break down each Mahadasha into Antardasha (level 2), Pratyantar (3), then Sūkṣma (4) and Prāṇa (5).  These levels subdivide each preceding period in fixed proportion (120-year cycle) using the formula: 

- **Sūkṣma (Level 4) Dasha:** (Pratyantar duration) × (planet’s full Mahadasha years) ÷ 120.  
- **Prāṇa (Level 5) Dasha:** (Sūkṣma duration) × (planet’s Mahadasha years) ÷ 120.  

In other words, each level’s length is obtained by multiplying the remaining length of the parent period by the sub-period ruler’s years and dividing by 120【46†L251-L253】.  (For example, after finding a Pratyantar Dasha span, you multiply that span by the Praṇa-lord’s full 120-year Dasha length and divide by 120 to get the Prāṇa span【46†L251-L253】.)  

*Swara Dasha (Level 6):*  Some authors mention a further division (sometimes called Swara or “Saatvik” Dasha), but it is seldom used in classical texts.  If applied, it follows the same pattern: each Level-6 Swara segment = (Prāṇa segment) × (planet’s Mahadasha years) ÷ 120.  In practice, Swara Dasha is rarely cited; Sūkṣma and Prāṇa levels already correspond to days/hours.

**Birth-time accuracy:**  The finer the subdivision, the more precisely the birth time must be known.  Vimshottari’s Mahadasha and Antar can usually be computed accurately to the year even if birth time is only approximate.  However, **for Pratyantar and smaller dashas the birth time must be known to seconds**【53†L165-L168】.  As a rule of thumb, if the birth time error is on the order of ±10–15 minutes, one should trust **only Mahadasha and Antar** (and perhaps a rough Pratyantar).  Sūkṣma (days/weeks) and Prāṇa (hours) will be unreliable under such uncertainty.  (IndAstro notes: “Mahadasha and Antardashas can be detected by birth date and approximate time.  However, for prediction of Pratyantar and further, exact birth time down to seconds is necessary”【53†L165-L168】.) 

**Typical events by level:**  In general, *Mahadasha/Antardasha/Pratyantar* govern major life events or year‐scale timings (career, marriage year, relocation, childbirth, etc.).  *Sūkṣma* dashas (days to weeks) are used for intermediate events like travel periods, surgeries, short-term projects, or onset of illness.  *Prāṇa* dashas (hours to fractions of a day) are invoked for the most precise events – e.g. the hour of an accident, the timing of death, the exact start of a ceremony.  One source notes that Sūkṣma dashas can pinpoint “marriage, travel, health issues, accidents, or spiritual transformations” on a fine scale【41†L55-L61】.  By contrast, Mahadasha/Antar/Pratyantar set the broad timeframe, while Sūkṣma/Prāṇa refine it to specific days or hours. 

**Sub-period sequence rule:**  At each level, the sub-dasha cycle always *restarts* with the planet of the parent period.  In other words, within a Mahadasha of Planet X, the first Antar is X, then proceed through the usual order.  Likewise, within an Antar of Planet Y, the first Pratyantar is Y, etc.  For example, if the Mahadasha is Saturn, then the first Antar is Saturn–Sun, the first Pratyantar of a Saturn–Sun Antar is Sun, and so on.  Each subdivision’s sequence begins with its own lord.

Chugh, et al.) have elaborated the nakshatra-planet mapping and practical guidelines【20†L393-L401】【30†L220-L228】.  Technical formulas (e.g. for Antar/Sūkṣma/Prāṇa) are implicit in these texts and modern books on dasha calculations【46†L251-L253】【53†L161-L168】.
