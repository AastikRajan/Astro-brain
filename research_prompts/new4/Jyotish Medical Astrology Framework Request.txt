Comprehensive Medical Astrology Framework in Classical Jyotish Shastra
Introduction to the Medical Jyotish Framework
The diagnostic and prognostic architecture of Ayur Jyotish (Vedic Medical Astrology) represents an intricate, mathematically rigid synthesis of astronomy, geometry, and Ayurvedic pathology. Derived from authoritative classical treatises such as the Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (BPHS), Prasna Marga, Brihat Jataka, Saravali, and Jataka Tattva, this framework eschews modern psychological archetypes in favor of computable principles of planetary dignity, positional geometry, and temporal activation. Within this ancient paradigm, human physiology is viewed as a direct microcosm of the celestial sphere, establishing a resonant link between the macrocosm (Brahmanda) and the physical body (Pindanda). Somatic and psychiatric ailments are interpreted strictly as manifestations of Prarabdha Karma (ripened past actions), activated through precise planetary chronometries known as Dashas (planetary periods) and transits (Gocharas).1
The classical texts categorize all human afflictions into two primary vectors: Nija (natural or internal diseases) and Agantuka (acquired or external diseases). Nija diseases are further subdivided into Sareerotha, which are purely physical ailments predominantly governed by the eighth house, and Chittotha, which encompass psychiatric and emotional disorders governed by the fifth and eighth houses. Agantuka diseases are either Drishta Nimittaja (ailments with known causes, such as physical trauma, infections, or accidents, governed by the sixth house) or Adrishta Nimittaja (ailments with unknown, unseen, or seemingly mystical causes, governed by the Badhaka houses).1 The practitioner's task is to compute the exact interplay of the tripartite variables—houses (Bhavas), planets (Grahas), and signs (Rashis)—to isolate the anatomical locus of the disease, define its pathological nature, and predict its onset, severity, and conclusion.
1. The Anatomical Zodiac: Detailed House-Body Mapping
The foundational diagnostic tool in Ayur Jyotish is the "Triple Mapping" matrix. A precise medical diagnosis requires the concurrent synthesis of the astrological House (representing the structural location within the individual's specific natal chart), the natural Zodiac Sign (representing the energetic and elemental environment or biological terrain), and the Planet (serving as the natural physiological significator or Karaka). When a specific house, its corresponding natural zodiac sign, and its ruling planetary significator are simultaneously afflicted by malefics—whether through conjunction, malefic aspect, or Papakartari Yoga (being hemmed in between two malefics)—the corresponding biological system undergoes pathological breakdown.2
The twelve houses of the horoscope map sequentially to the human body, proceeding topographically from the cranium down to the plantar surfaces of the feet. The first house (Lagna) dictates the cranium, cerebral cortex, face, overall physical constitution, and baseline immunological vitality. The second house governs the right eye, oral cavity, dentition, tongue, pharynx, thyroid, and the cervical region. The third house commands the clavicles, shoulders, upper extremities, bronchial tubes, and the peripheral nervous system. The fourth house represents the cardiac muscle, myocardium, mammary glands, pleura, and pulmonary structures. The fifth house rules the upper abdomen, gastric mucosa, hepatic system, gallbladder, pancreas, and the cognitive intellect (Buddhi). The sixth house dictates the lower abdomen, small intestines, renal system, and the biological terrain for acute infections. The seventh house governs the pelvic girdle, groin, internal reproductive organs, and prostate. The eighth house rules the rectum, excretory systems, perineum, and serves as the primary locus for chronic, terminal, or incurable pathologies. The ninth house commands the femoral region, hips, thighs, and the arterial system. The tenth house rules the patella, knee joints, and the structural skeletal system. The eleventh house dictates the tibia, fibula, calves, ankles, and the systemic circulatory network. Finally, the twelfth house governs the feet, toes, lymphatic system, and conditions requiring profound confinement or hospitalization.2
The planets govern specific biological tissues (Dhatus), physiological systems, and Ayurvedic humours (Tridoshas). The Sun is the Karaka for the heart, skeletal system, right eye, and overall vitality, governing the Pitta (bilious) humour. The Moon rules the mind, left eye, bodily fluids, and mammary glands, governing the Kapha (phlegmatic) humour. Mars signifies blood, bone marrow, musculature, and adrenaline, also governing Pitta. Mercury rules the central nervous system, skin, and respiratory bronchial tubes, overseeing all three humours (Tridosha). Jupiter governs adipose tissue, the liver, gallbladder, and the pancreas, strongly tied to Kapha. Venus rules the reproductive system, renal function, and seminal fluids, governing a mix of Kapha and Vata. Saturn is the significator for joints, teeth, cartilage, and cellular aging, governing the Vata (airy) humour. Rahu signifies undiagnosed ailments, virology, oncology, and poisoning, while Ketu signifies parasitic infections, epidemic outbreaks, and sudden traumatic injuries.2

	2. Disease Timing and Planetary Dasha Activation
The manifestation of congenital vulnerabilities or acquired diseases remains dormant until triggered by the precise chronological activation of the Vimshottari Dasha (planetary periods). In medical Jyotish, the lord of the 6th house is the primary activator of acute illnesses, fevers, and immune distress. The lord of the 8th house triggers chronic, deeply debilitating, and potentially fatal diseases. The lord of the 12th house necessitates profound physical confinement, hospitalization, or prolonged bed rest.6
The dignity of the Dasha lord dictates the absolute severity of the pathology. A planet situated in its sign of exaltation (Ucha), its own sign (Swakshetra), or occupying friendly Navamsas will produce mild, curable, or highly transient symptoms that respond rapidly to medical intervention. Conversely, a planet operating in deep debilitation (Neecha), combust (Astangata) by proximity to the Sun, or occupying enemy signs will produce Aristaphala—severe, intractable miseries, chronic suffering, and highly complex pathologies that resist standard medical treatment.19
Specific Disease Activations by Dasha Lord
* Sun Dasha: Activation of solar afflictions during its period leads to severe hyperthermia, cardiac arrhythmias, visual disturbances (specifically in the right eye), migraine clusters, calcium deficiencies, and osseous (bone) degeneration.9
* Moon Dasha: Afflicted lunar periods bring profound emotional morbidity, psychiatric disturbances, severe insomnia, hormonal turbulence, lymphatic stagnation, pulmonary edema, and breast-related pathologies.9
* Mars Dasha: Mars governs impulse and heat. Its dasha triggers acute medical crises, including severe febrile illnesses, traumatic hemorrhage, sudden and unavoidable surgical interventions, hematological toxicity, aggressive inflammatory cascades, and severe hypertension.9
* Mercury Dasha: Mercurial afflictions result in systemic neurological breakdowns, aphasia and speech impediments, severe dermatological eruptions, intense asthmatic episodes, vertigo, and peripheral sensory neuropathy.9
* Jupiter Dasha: Afflicted Jupiter triggers diseases of expansion and excess. Its periods mark the rapid onset of diabetes mellitus, hepatic steatosis (fatty liver), morbid obesity, cholelithiasis (gallstones), organ hypertrophy, and systemic lipemia.9
* Venus Dasha: Venusian periods trigger renal insufficiencies, severe genitourinary tract infections, diminished virility, autoimmune disorders related to bodily fluids, ocular cataracts, and venereal diseases.9
* Saturn Dasha: Saturn represents the principle of chronicity and blockages. Its dasha initiates arthritic calcification, profound physical exhaustion, paralytic episodes, oncological slow-growth formations, spinal degradation, and deep-seated, treatment-resistant melancholia.9
* Rahu Dasha: The North Node triggers completely undiagnosed or misdiagnosed pathologies, aggressive malignant neoplasms, illnesses due to exposure to environmental toxins or poisons, auto-immune destruction, and severe psychological neuroses or hallucinatory episodes.9
* Ketu Dasha: The South Node initiates sudden, sharp, inexplicable pains, localized viral or bacterial epidemics, deep ulcerations, intestinal parasitic infections, and sudden neuromuscular atrophy.9
3. The Maraka and Badhaka Systems: Vectors of Mortality and Obstruction
When examining extreme medical crises, classical Jyotish enforces a strict differentiation between periods of temporary affliction, chronic lifelong suffering, and terminal exit. This crucial distinction is governed by the intricate mathematical identification of the Maraka (death-inflicting) and Badhaka (obstruction-causing) planets within the natal chart.
The Maraka Framework
The calculation of mortality relies on the principle of Bhavat Bhavam (house to house relation). The 8th house governs the absolute, mathematically allocated quotient of longevity (Ayurdaya). Consequently, the 3rd house, being the 8th house counted from the 8th house, serves as the secondary house of vitality and lifespan. Furthermore, the 12th house from any given house represents the absolute destruction, expenditure, or loss of its core significations. Therefore, the 12th house from the 3rd is the 2nd house, and the 12th house from the 8th is the 7th house. Thus, the 2nd and 7th houses, and specifically their ruling planets, are designated as the primary Marakas (death-inflicting agents).18
Planets located physically within the 2nd or 7th houses, or those closely conjunct the Maraka lords, acquire potent secondary Maraka properties. Saturn, as the natural universal significator of death and decay, becomes an undeniable terminal agent if it associates with any Maraka lord, overriding other protective influences.23
The Badhaka Framework
While Marakas manage the natural end of life, Badhakas represent unseen obstacles, severe medical misdiagnoses, sudden physical trauma, and the wrath of unseen forces. The determination of the Badhaka lord depends entirely on the modality of the individual's Ascendant (Lagna) sign.25

Differentiating Death vs. Severe Illness vs. Financial Loss
A common, highly amateur fallacy in astrological diagnosis is predicting death merely because a Maraka Mahadasha or Antardasha is operating. The classical texts insist that a three-fold concurrent activation matrix must be entirely fulfilled for physical death to transpire 18:
1. Dasha Activation: The native must be running the Mahadasha or Antardasha of a primary Maraka or Badhaka planet, or the 8th lord sub-dasha operating under a Maraka main period.29
2. Transit Trigger: Potent malefic transits (specifically Saturn, Rahu, or Ketu) must be simultaneously crossing death-sensitive points in the chart, such as the natal Ascendant, the natal Moon, or the 8th house.29
3. Longevity Exhaustion: Most critically, the mathematically computed lifespan (Ayurdaya) must have reached its predetermined structural conclusion.18
Resolution Rules for Non-Lethal Maraka Periods: If conditions 1 and 2 are met, but the Ayurdaya calculation (condition 3) indicates that several years of lifespan remain, the Maraka planet will not kill. Instead, its destructive energy is diverted into causing severe, near-fatal illness, prolonged hospitalization, or crippling physical accidents that the native ultimately survives.18 Furthermore, if the Maraka planet is entirely unafflicted by the lords of the 6th, 8th, or 12th houses, and the native is in their youth or prime working years, the energy of the Maraka (which inherently rules the 2nd house of wealth or the 7th house of partnerships) bypasses the physical body altogether. It manifests instead as catastrophic financial losses, total bankruptcy, or the hostile dissolution of marriage.18
4. Mathematical Computation of Longevity (Ayurdaya)
To apply the Maraka rules with any degree of accuracy, the astrologer must first compute the base longevity of the native. Jyotish divides the human lifespan into three primary tranches: Alpa Ayus (Short Life: 0–36 years), Madhya Ayus (Medium Life: 36–72 years), and Purna Ayus (Long Life: 72–108+ years).31 The texts outline highly specific mathematical models: Pindayu, Amsayu, and Nisargayu, along with the categorical Jaimini Three Pairs method.
The Decision Tree for Method Selection
According to BPHS Chapter 43, the choice of the primary mathematical method relies on calculating the exact planetary strengths (Shadbala) to find the most powerful entity among the Ascendant (Lagna), the Sun, and the Moon 36:
* If the Sun is the strongest of the three    The Pindayu method is mandated.37
* If the Lagna is the strongest of the three    The Amsayu method is mandated.37
* If the Moon is the strongest of the three    The Nisargayu method is mandated.38
* Conflict Resolution: If two entities are of perfectly equal mathematical strength, both respective methods are calculated, and the resulting ages are averaged together. If all three entities are of equal strength, the mathematical average of all three methods provides the final Ayurdaya.36
The Pindayu Formula and Mechanics
Under the Pindayu system, planets grant a maximum predetermined quota of years when occupying their exact degree of deep exaltation (Ucha): Sun grants 19 years, Moon 25 years, Mars 15 years, Mercury 12 years, Jupiter 15 years, Venus 21 years, and Saturn 20 years.37 When a planet resides in its exact degree of deep debilitation (Neecha), this contribution is cut exactly in half (e.g., Sun grants 9.5 years).37
For planetary placements falling anywhere between these two absolute points, the mathematical Rule of Three is strictly applied 38:

   4. Compute the Base Age (Graha Aayu): Multiply the Full Pindayu Years assigned to the planet by the Effective Arc, and divide by   . This fractional multiplier scales the years smoothly between full exaltation and half debilitation.38
The Nisargayu and Amsayu Variations
The Nisargayu method utilizes the exact same mathematical computations, effective arcs, and reduction rules as Pindayu, but alters the maximum years granted at exaltation based on natural biological stages: Sun grants 20 years, Moon 1 year, Mars 2 years, Mercury 9 years, Jupiter 18 years, Venus 20 years, and Saturn 50 years.38
The Amsayu method derives longevity fundamentally from the Navamsa (1/9th division) placements. The full Nirayana longitude of the planet is converted into minutes and divided by 200 (the exact size of a Navamsa in minutes). Multiples of 12 are expunged, and the remainder indicates the base age contributed by the planet.40 Crucially, before reductions are applied, Amsayu requires Bharanas (additions). If a planet is Exalted, Retrograde, or in its Own Sign, its base years are multiplied by 3. If it is Vargottama or situated in its Own Navamsa/Drekkana, it is multiplied by 2.38
Harana (Reductions) Rules
The base age derived from these methods must be subjected to specific geometric and dignity-based reductions known as Haranas 37:
      * Astangata Harana (Combustion): If a planet is mathematically combust by proximity to the Sun, its granted years are halved (multiply by 0.5). Venus and Saturn are exempt from this reduction.37
      * Shatrukshetra Harana (Enemy Sign): If a planet is occupying a natural enemy's sign, its granted years are reduced by exactly one-third (multiply by 0.666). Mars and any retrograde planets are strictly exempt.37
      * Chakrapata Harana (Visible Half): Malefics located in the visible hemisphere (Houses 7 through 12) suffer severe, tiered deductions: placement in the 12th house results in full loss of years; 11th house loses 1/2; 10th house loses 1/3; 9th house loses 1/4; 8th house loses 1/5; and 7th house loses 1/6. Benefic planets in these same houses lose exactly half of what malefics would lose.38
      * Krurodaya Harana: A malefic planet occupying the Ascendant causes a proportional reduction to the final sum of all planetary ages, determined precisely by the Lagna's exact longitude.37

5. Arishta Yoga: Infant Mortality and its Cancellations
Even if the extensive mathematical Ayurdaya indicates a lengthy lifespan, the presence of Arishta Yogas (severe afflictions to the newborn) can truncate life entirely or cause profound, life-altering early illness. In Jyotish, danger to youth is strictly segmented into Balarishta (death or extreme suffering before age 8), Madhyarishta (ages 8 to 20), and Yogarishta (ages 20 to 32).48 Classical computations of exact longevity are prohibited from being finalized or declared until the child safely passes the 8-year Balarishta threshold, as volatile karmic anomalies dominate this early period.48
Primary Balarishta Conditions
Immediate danger to the infant's life is diagnosed through the following severe structural afflictions 49:
      1. Lunar Affliction in Dusthanas: The Moon located in the 6th, 8th, or 12th house, entirely devoid of benefic aspects, and flanked or aspected exclusively by powerful malefic planets.49
      2. Papakartari Lagna: The Ascendant or the natal Moon caught tightly between two severe malefics (e.g., Saturn in the 12th house and Mars in the 2nd house) with absolutely no benefic relief or aspect.49
      3. Eclipse Births: Birth occurring during the exact window of a solar or lunar eclipse where the Ascendant is simultaneously occupied or aspected by Saturn or Mars.49
      4. Lagna Lord Defeat: The Ascendant Lord situated in the 7th house, undergoing Graha Yuddha (planetary war by exact degree conjunction) with a malefic planet, and losing the battle.50
Balarishta Bhanga (Absolute Cancellation Rules)
The destructive, terminal force of infant mortality yogas is entirely nullified (Bhanga) if specific, highly potent protective configurations are present in the natal chart 49:
      1. The Jupiterian Shield: Jupiter possessing high directional strength (Digbala) occupying the 1st house (Lagna) singularly destroys all Balarishta, acting as an absolute shield against early death.49
      2. Strong Lagna Lord: The Ascendant lord occupying an angular house (Kendra: 1, 4, 7, or 10), placed in a friendly sign or its exaltation, and receiving the aspect of natural benefics.49
      3. Lunar Dignity: A Full Moon (waxing, near opposition to the Sun) located in a benefic sign or Navamsa, receiving the unbroken, exact aspect of Jupiter.51
      4. Temporal Cancellation: An infant born during the daytime in the dark lunar fortnight (Krishna Paksha), or alternatively born during the nighttime in the bright lunar fortnight (Shukla Paksha), is naturally shielded from lunar-induced Balarishta, providing an easy escape even if the Moon resides in the highly dangerous 8th house.34

	7. Psychiatric and Mental Health Diagnostics
In Jyotish, psychiatric illness (Chittotha) is not viewed as a mere imbalance of humours, but a profound disruption of the internal cognitive architecture. It is primarily evaluated through the complex intersection of the Moon (ruling the emotional mind and subconscious, Manas), Mercury (ruling the nervous system, cognitive processing, and neurological wiring), the 4th house (emotional equilibrium and inner peace), and the 5th house (intellect, judgment, and Buddhi).1
Classical Yogas for Mental Illness
      1. Depression and Melancholia: This manifests most distinctly via Kemadruma Yoga—a highly detrimental condition where the Moon is completely isolated in the chart, lacking planetary conjunctions and having no planets in the immediately preceding and succeeding houses. If such an isolated Moon receives the aspect of Saturn without any benefic intervention, the native suffers from profound neurochemical depression, isolation, lack of emotional support, and chronic, heavy anxiety.7
      2. Psychosis and Schizophrenia: Classical aphorisms in the Jataka Tattva isolate highly severe spatial arrangements for insanity. Jupiter positioned in the Ascendant exactly opposed by Mars in the 7th house (or vice versa) violently disrupts the cognitive axis. Alternatively, Saturn occupying the Ascendant while the Sun is sequestered in the 12th house, accompanied by malefic aspects to a weak Moon, indicates severe dissociative disorders, violent outbursts, or complete lunacy.55

10. Procreative Astrology: Fertility, Conception, and Gender
The assessment of biological fertility and the architectural timing of conception form the foundation of procreative Ayur Jyotish. Structural infertility is diagnosed not merely by looking at afflictions to the 5th house, but through highly specialized, mathematically derived summations known as Beeja Sphuta (representing the male seed) and Kshetra Sphuta (representing the female field/womb).72
Beeja and Kshetra Sphuta Computation
            1. Beeja Sphuta (Male Virility): Computed by adding the exact longitudes of the Sun (representing vitality and the father), Venus (representing the seminal fluid), and Jupiter (the universal natural significator of progeny).
  
               * Interpretation Rule: The resulting combined longitudinal point establishes a specific Sign (Rashi) and Navamsa. If both the Rashi and Navamsa are Odd/Masculine signs (Aries, Gemini, Leo, Libra, Sagittarius, Aquarius), male virility is biologically sound and potent. If both fall in Even/Feminine signs, there is a profound lack of virility, indicating clinical infertility. A mixed result (one odd, one even) indicates delayed progeny requiring medical treatment, astrological remedies, or extended effort.72
               2. Kshetra Sphuta (Female Fecundity): Computed by adding the exact longitudes of the Moon (representing the menstrual cycle and ovulation), Mars (representing uterine blood), and Jupiter (progeny).
  
                  * Interpretation Rule: If the resulting point falls in Even/Feminine signs in both the Rashi and Navamsa, the female reproductive system is highly fertile and the "soil" is ready. If both fall in Odd/Masculine signs, there is a severe risk of barrenness, polycystic issues, or chronic uterine hostility to implantation. A mixed result indicates complications, ectopic risks, or significantly delayed conception.73
Adhana Lagna (Conception Timing)
To track embryonic development, screen for congenital abnormalities, and confirm the legitimacy of birth timing, classical texts such as BPHS and Brihat Jataka establish the Adhana Lagna or Nisheka Lagna (the astrological chart of the exact moment of conception/implantation).
The primary method involves calculating backward from the exact birth time. The standard gestational constant utilized in Jyotish is 273 days (exactly 10 sidereal lunar months). However, the exact timing is mathematically refined by assessing the angular distance between the natal Ascendant and the 9th house, generating the precise degree of the conception ascendant.77 Malefic placements in this Adhana chart—such as Saturn occupying the Lagna, or malefics crowding the 12th house without any benefic aspects—point directly to severe complications during gestation, restricted fetal growth, or intrauterine abnormalities.77
