Comprehensive Analytical Report on the Mechanics, Manifestation, and Quantification of Astrological Yogas
Introduction to Planetary Synthesis and the Illusion of Abundance
In the advanced practice of Vedic Astrology (Jyotish), the identification of planetary combinations, known as Yogas, forms the structural foundation of horoscopic analysis. A Yoga represents a specific mathematical, angular, and spatial union of planets, signs, and houses that produces distinct, predictable outcomes in human life.1 When utilizing modern computational software, it is exceedingly common to detect upwards of three hundred distinct yogas within a single natal chart.3 However, this sheer volume creates an illusion of abundance that often misleads practitioners. The presence of a yoga in a static birth chart represents mere potential energy, a latent karmic blueprint that may or may not ever actualize in the material world.
The actualization of this potential—its transition from abstract astronomical geometry to tangible life events—is governed by a highly complex, multi-layered matrix of chronological triggers, spatial modifiers, divisional dignities, and conflicting planetary algorithms.3 A chart boasting hundreds of yogas does not guarantee a life of extraordinary achievement; rather, it necessitates a rigorous filtering mechanism to determine which combinations possess the structural integrity to manifest, when they will execute their promised results, and how they interact with overlapping or contradictory forces within the horoscope.3
The exhaustive analysis presented herein addresses the functional realities of astrological yogas, dispensing with theoretical platitudes to examine empirical reliability, manifestation timelines, cancellation principles, and the mathematical quantification of compounding planetary combinations. The subsequent sections systematically map the transition of yogas from abstract potential to dynamic reality, providing a rigorous, domain-specific grading system and analytical framework designed for advanced astrological application. By dissecting the mechanics of grading, timing, cancellation, and logarithmic compounding, this report establishes a definitive methodology for evaluating the true potency of any birth chart.
Part 1: Evaluative Grading of Yogas and Manifestation Reliability
Not all astrological yogas carry equal weight, nor do they possess a uniform probability of manifestation. The reliability of a yoga is entirely contingent upon foundational planetary dignities, angular momentum, the absence of structural afflictions, and the inherent strength of the Ascendant (Lagna) lord. Evaluating the true efficacy of a yoga requires moving beyond its basic definition and subjecting it to rigorous stress-testing across multiple astronomical variables.
Pancha Mahapurusha Yogas: Statistical Reliability and Failure Rates
The Pancha Mahapurusha Yogas—Hamsa (Jupiter), Malavya (Venus), Ruchaka (Mars), Bhadra (Mercury), and Sasa (Saturn)—are lauded in classical texts as supreme combinations that elevate an individual to greatness, endowing them with the highest qualities of the respective planet.3 They form when one of the five non-luminary planets occupies a Kendra (angular house: 1st, 4th, 7th, or 10th) from the Ascendant or Moon, while simultaneously residing in its own or exalted sign.3
In practical application, the reliability of these yogas is highly variable and prone to significant failure rates if secondary conditions are not stringently met. They do not invariably deliver exceptional results, and their presence is not a guaranteed ticket to prominence.8 For a Pancha Mahapurusha Yoga to function as a highly reliable, infallible predictor of success, several precise technical criteria must overlap seamlessly. Primarily, the planet must possess both positional dignity (Sthana Bala) through exaltation or rulership, and angular strength (Kendra Bala) by occupying a quadrant.3 However, this is merely the baseline. The specific degree of the planet significantly dictates its capability.3 Planets positioned in the structural sandhi (junctions) of a sign—specifically between zero and five degrees, or twenty-five and thirty degrees—lack the energetic capacity to manifest the yoga's full potential, operating instead in an infant or senile state.3 The utmost reliability is consistently observed when the yoga-forming planet occupies the middle decanate, roughly between ten and twenty degrees, where its expression is most robust.3
Furthermore, the most common reason a technically perfect Pancha Mahapurusha Yoga fails in practice is its hidden affliction in the D9 (Navamsa) divisional chart.3 The Navamsa acts as the internal reality or the fruit of the D1 (Rashi) chart's tree. If a yoga-forming planet is debilitated, combust, or heavily afflicted by malefics in the Navamsa, the results in the physical world will remain profoundly weak, diluted, or entirely latent, regardless of its brilliant placement in the primary birth chart.3 Additionally, if the planet is combust (Asta) by being too close to the Sun, or heavily aspected by natural malefics without intervening benefic aspects, the structural integrity of the yoga is compromised.3 When all these conditions—optimal degree, Navamsa strength, and freedom from severe affliction—are flawlessly met, the reliability of a Pancha Mahapurusha Yoga approaches near-certainty. In such pristine conditions, individuals often display exceptional qualities in the domains governed by the planet even prior to the formal activation of its planetary period.3
Raja Yogas: The Latency Versus Manifestation Dichotomy
Raja Yogas represent the ultimate synthesis of purpose and action in Vedic astrology. They predominantly form when the lords of the Kendra houses (representing karma, action, and material manifestation) intersect with the lords of the Trikona houses (representing dharma, inherent purpose, and divine grace) through conjunction, mutual aspect, or the exchange of signs.9 These are the primary indicators of authority, lasting fame, kingly status, and high achievement.9
Despite their heralded status, empirical observation reveals a stark latency versus manifestation dichotomy. An overwhelming percentage of Raja Yogas—estimated by advanced practitioners to be upwards of eighty to ninety percent—remain perpetually latent or operate only at a marginal background level. The manifestation of a Raja Yoga is absolutely contingent upon the Vimshottari Dasha timeline.10 Because the human lifespan rarely covers the entirety of the 120-year Vimshottari cycle, a perfectly constructed Raja Yoga will fail to produce worldly authority if the Dasha (major period) of the yoga-forming planets never activates during the native's productive adult years.10 A perfectly formed Raja Yoga that only enters its operational phase when the native is eighty years old, or conversely, when the native is an infant, will not yield the kingly professional success the texts promise.10
Moreover, the manifestation percentage is further throttled by the strength of the Ascendant. A weak Ascendant (Lagna) lord serves as a severe bottleneck; it undermines the native's physical and psychological capacity to wield, sustain, and utilize the immense power generated by a Raja Yoga.10 Therefore, while Raja Yogas are theoretically powerful, their actual manifestation rate is statistically low, requiring the precise alignment of Dasha timing during prime life phases and a resilient physical vessel (Lagna) to bear the fruit.
Dhana Yogas: Actualizing Wealth and Fiscal Potential
Dhana Yogas dictate the accumulation, retention, and exponential expansion of wealth.2 The foundational Dhana Yoga requires an intimate connection between the second house, representing accumulated wealth and savings, and the eleventh house, representing liquid gains and regular income.12 More potent variations, such as the Maha Dhan Yoga or Lakshmi Yoga variants, involve the highly auspicious Trikona houses (the fifth and ninth) intersecting with the primary wealth houses, thereby merging intelligence, creative merit, and divine fortune with financial accumulation.12
In practice, Dhana Yogas boast a very high reliability rate regarding the production of wealth, provided they are not severely afflicted by the lords of the dusthana houses (the sixth, eighth, and twelfth).10 However, it is crucial to temper expectations: foundational Dhana Yogas rarely produce sudden, lottery-style, instantaneous riches. Instead, they represent a progressive, compounding fiscal trajectory characterized by a steady income flow that reliably converts to savings.12 The actual magnitude of the wealth produced—whether it equates to comfortable middle-class stability or staggering billionaire status—is dictated by the divisional dignities (Vaiseshikamsa) of the planets involved and their strength in the Ashtakavarga system.14 Sudden, massive influxes of wealth are usually reserved for charts that combine strong Dhana Yogas with specific eighth house activations or Vipreet Raj Yoga dynamics, which govern unearned wealth, inheritances, and sudden transformations.16
Nabhasa Yogas: Foundational Modifiers Versus Active Predictors
Nabhasa Yogas represent a distinct and often misunderstood class of astrological phenomena. Comprising four main categories—Akrati (diagrammatical), Sankhya (numerical), Asraya (positional), and Dala (bilateral)—these yogas are defined by the overarching geometric arrangement of all seven visible planets in the celestial realm, irrespective of specific house lordships or conjunctions.18 Ancient texts, such as those by the Yavanas, detail thousands of these overarching planetary patterns.18
Unlike Raja or Dhana Yogas, which lie entirely dormant until explicitly activated by a specific planetary period, Nabhasa Yogas function as constant, pervasive background modifiers.18 They are structurally woven into the individual's psyche, representing the Sanchita Karma (accumulated actions of past lives) that shapes the native's baseline constitution.19 The analytical evidence suggests that Nabhasa Yogas are decidedly weak predictors of specific, timed events. They will not predict a sudden promotion, a marriage, or a financial windfall. Instead, they act as strong foundational indicators that dictate the overarching thematic pattern of the native's life.18 They reveal inherent psychological inclinations, basic character strengths, and fundamental weaknesses that operate continuously from birth to death, entirely independent of the active Vimshottari Dasha.18 As such, they should be utilized to understand the native's enduring nature rather than to time specific manifestations.
Arishta and Balarishta Yogas: Prognosticating Health and Longevity
Arishta Yogas, translating literally to "malign" or "misfortune," are critical astrological indicators of morbidity, physical suffering, mental anguish, and potential early mortality, which is specifically termed Balarishta when occurring in infancy.20 These detrimental yogas primarily form when the Ascendant or its lord is severely weakened and relegated to the Dusthana houses (the sixth, eighth, or twelfth), or when the Moon, which governs the mind and early childhood vitality, is heavily afflicted by natural malefics without any benefic support.21
In the context of modern medical astrology, these yogas are highly reliable for identifying inherent physiological vulnerabilities, congenital defects, and periods of severe somatic stress.22 However, their fatalistic predictions regarding longevity must be weighed against the principle of cancellation (Balarishta Bhanga). The ancient texts place immense emphasis on the protective capacity of natural benefics. A strongly placed Jupiter, Venus, or Mercury—especially if exalted, in its own sign, or vargottama—positioned in the Ascendant acts as an absolute prophylactic.21 Such benefic placements effectively nullify the life-threatening aspects of the Arishta Yoga, converting what might have been a fatal outcome into a manageable health challenge.21 Therefore, while Arishta Yogas reliably point to periods of physical vulnerability and required medical intervention, their ultimate reliability as predictors of mortality is frequently overruled by the presence of strong benefic interventions elsewhere in the chart.
Part 2: The Chronology of Yoga Delivery – Dashas and Transits
The most profound and complex architecture within a birth chart remains entirely dormant without the chronological activation provided by the planetary period system (Dasha) and the spatial triggering mechanisms of planetary transits (Gochara).4 Understanding the interplay between these two temporal systems is the cornerstone of accurate astrological prediction.
The Dasha Activation Imperative and Its Exceptions
The paramount rule of Vedic astrology dictates that a yoga delivers its promised results exclusively when the yoga-forming planet runs its Mahadasha (major period) or Antardasha (sub-period).5 This rule is nearly absolute for material events such as wealth acquisition, career elevation, and marriage.10 The Dasha acts as the karmic blueprint, indicating the specific energy that is authorized to dominate a chronological epoch of the native's life.3 Without this authorization, planetary combinations remain in a state of suspended potentiality.5
However, there are highly specific exceptions to this rule. As previously noted, Nabhasa Yogas operate continuously regardless of the Dasha.18 Furthermore, if a yoga-forming planet does not run its own Dasha, the results of the yoga can sometimes be activated during the Dasha of the planet that acts as the sign lord (depositor) of the yoga-forming planets, provided that depositor is exceptionally strong.10 Additionally, nodes (Rahu and Ketu) will deliver the results of the planets they are conjoined with, effectively activating the yogas of their conjunct partners during their own extended periods.14
The Symbiotic Interplay of Dashas and Gochara (Transits)
The relationship between Dashas and Transits is fundamentally symbiotic, yet hierarchical. The Dasha provides the foundational promise and the macro-level timeline, while the transit acts as the localized, real-time trigger that grounds the event into physical reality.4 Analyzing scenarios where these two forces conflict provides profound insight into the nuance of yoga delivery.
When the yoga-forming planet is running its Dasha, the fundamental promise of the yoga is mandated to deliver. However, if the current planetary transits—especially the slow-moving transits of Saturn and Rahu—are overwhelmingly hostile, the delivery of the yoga's results will be characterized by extreme environmental friction, frustrating delays, and intense psychological stress.4 For example, if a native is running a highly favorable Jupiter Dasha promising career elevation, but transiting Saturn is crossing over their natal Moon (Sade Sati) or afflicting the tenth house, the promotion will still occur because the Dasha mandates it. However, the native may experience intense anxiety, overwork, and hostility from superiors during the process. The yoga delivers, but the subjective experience of the delivery is arduous.26
Conversely, consider a scenario where transits are exceptionally supportive, but the active Dasha is unrelated or hostile. If Jupiter transits the tenth house of career—a traditionally excellent transit for professional growth—but the active Mahadasha is governed by an afflicted twelfth house lord indicating loss and isolation, the favorable transit cannot override the Dasha's structural mandate.4 A transit alone lacks the authority to manifest a major life event that the active Dasha does not support. In such cases, the auspicious transit will not deliver a Raja Yoga; it may merely mitigate the ongoing difficulties of the twelfth lord Dasha, provide a fleeting sense of optimism, or offer a minor, temporary opportunity that ultimately fails to result in a permanent structural elevation.4
Antardasha (Bhukti) Efficacy in Isolation
A persistent analytical question among practitioners is whether a yoga can deliver its results during the Antardasha (Bhukti) of a yoga-forming planet even if the overarching Mahadasha lord is entirely unrelated to the yoga or occupies a hostile position. The astrological consensus confirms that delivery does occur, but within strictly enforced parameters.5
The Antardasha operates entirely within the boundaries and overarching agenda established by the Mahadasha lord.5 If a powerful Dhana Yoga-forming planet enters its Antardasha during the Mahadasha of a neutral or mildly malefic planet, it will indeed produce sudden financial gains, luxurious acquisitions, and distinct opportunities.5 However, the "ceiling" of this success is restricted by the Mahadasha. The yoga's delivery will be episodic, fleeting, and highly compartmentalized. It lacks the sustained, decade-long elevation and structural permanence that occurs when the Mahadasha itself is governed by the primary yoga-forming entity.5 The wealth gained during this sub-period may be substantial, but it must be carefully managed, as the underlying Dasha may not support its long-term retention.
Temporal Delay: Manifestation Timelines Within Dashas
A common misconception is that when a highly anticipated Dasha begins, the promised results manifest immediately on the exact date of calculation. In reality, the energetic transition between major planetary periods is gradual, overlapping, and subject to maturation.28
Analysis of manifestation timelines reveals that the subtle psychological and environmental shifts indicative of a new Dasha often begin a "fade-in" transition three to six months prior to the mathematical onset of the period.28 The final Antardasha of the preceding Mahadasha (the Dasha Chidra) often acts as a preview, setting the stage for the incoming energy.28 For massive combinations like a primary Raja Yoga or an intricate Maha Dhan Yoga, it typically requires a minimum of two years into the Mahadasha for the macro-level results to fully crystallize and visibly alter the native's life trajectory.28 The planet requires time to align with supportive Antardashas and must often wait for a favorable major transit of Jupiter or Saturn to fully ground the energetic promise into physical reality.5 Furthermore, research by eminent astrologers like K.N. Rao suggests that the profound effects of specific yogas—particularly spiritual and destiny-altering ones—are often timed precisely to the exact moments when transiting Jupiter or Saturn aspects the natal nodal axis (Rahu/Ketu), acting as the final temporal trigger within the Dasha window.29
Part 3: Yoga Cancellation and Conflicting Paradigms in Practice
Horoscopic analysis frequently encounters contradicting combinations that challenge straightforward interpretation. A single chart may hold both immense wealth indicators and abject poverty markers, or a planet may be exalted in one metric but debilitated or combust in another. Understanding the intricate mechanics of cancellation (Bhanga) and conflict resolution is essential for moving beyond superficial chart reading to accurate prognostication.
Neecha Bhanga Raja Yoga: The "Liability to Asset" Transformation
When a planet is placed in its sign of debilitation (Neecha), its core significations and the houses it rules are heavily compromised. This placement traditionally results in social censure, psychological dysfunction, chronic underperformance, or physical weakness.30 However, if specific astronomical and spatial conditions are met, this debility is structurally cancelled, forming a powerful Neecha Bhanga Raja Yoga.30
For this cancellation to be practically effective and not merely theoretical, strict rules apply. The foundational requirement is that the debilitated planet must occupy a Kendra (quadrant) from the Ascendant or the Moon, with the tenth house considered the most potent position for worldly success.30 Secondly, it must be intimately connected—usually through conjunction or mutual aspect—with a planet that is either exalted (Uchcha) or occupying its own sign.30 The presence of the exaltation lord of the debilitated sign in a quadrant also fulfills this requirement.30
The practical manifestation of Neecha Bhanga Raja Yoga operates on a profound psychological and karmic axiom: "First the neecha, then the bhanga".30 The cancellation does not retroactively erase the initial debility or grant an easy path; rather, it utilizes the trauma, deficit, or social humiliation as the exact catalyst required for future greatness.30 The native endures significant developmental delays, poverty, or embarrassment in the domain of the debilitated planet before the correction activates, ultimately turning a massive, highly visible liability into a spectacular, civilization-altering asset.30
This dynamic is perfectly illustrated through exhaustive historical case studies.30 Albert Einstein possessed a debilitated Mercury (governing analytical intellect and communication) in the tenth house of his chart. The initial neecha effect was profound: he experienced severe early developmental delays, did not speak until age four, and was dismissed by educators as unteachable. However, this debilitated Mercury was conjoined with an exalted Venus in a powerful quadrant. This activated a profound bhanga, transforming his unique, visually-oriented, "spacey" cognition into the very mechanism that birthed the General Theory of Relativity, fundamentally shifting the paradigm of global physics.30
Similarly, the author Stephen King was born with a debilitated Venus in the first house. The neecha manifestation resulted in a young adulthood defined by acute poverty, substance misuse, and severe physical trauma. Yet, this Venus was conjoined with an exalted Mercury. The resulting cancellation utilized the dark themes of his early life to generate staggering literary wealth, heavily supported by his spouse (represented by Venus), who served as the crucial stabilizing force in his ascent to prominence.30 Other global figures, such as Queen Elizabeth II (debilitated Jupiter conjunct exalted Mars) and Harry Truman (debilitated Mars conjunct exalted Jupiter), demonstrate this exact pattern of early restraint or perceived inadequacy giving way to extraordinary, world-shaping authority.30
The Confluence of Dhana Yogas and Daridra Yogas
When a natal chart contains both a Dhana Yoga (wealth production) and a Daridra Yoga (poverty, debt, and financial instability), they do not simply cancel each other out linearly to create a life of uninterrupted mediocrity.27 Instead, they operate sequentially or contextually based on their spatial placement and chronological Dasha activation.27
Advanced astrological application resolves this conflict by dividing the horoscope into two primary sectors to determine the trajectory of the native's financial life: the Dhan Bhag (Wealth Portion) and the Daridra Bhag (Poverty Portion).32 The Dhan Bhag comprises the first, second, ninth, tenth, eleventh, and twelfth houses. The Daridra Bhag comprises the third, fourth, fifth, sixth, seventh, and eighth houses.32
The interaction between the yoga and the sector determines the outcome:
* Dhana Yoga in the Dhan Bhag: This represents the most seamless manifestation. The individual generally experiences financial stability and good wealth from early life, continuing progressively.32
* Daridra Yoga in the Dhan Bhag: If a poverty-inducing combination sits in the wealth sector, it creates a trajectory of decline. The native is typically born into a wealthy family or achieves early success, but experiences a severe loss of fortune later in life, ending up in a difficult financial state.32
* Dhana Yoga in the Daridra Bhag: Conversely, if the wealth-producing combination is relegated to the poverty sector, it creates a trajectory of delayed ascent. The native usually originates from a background of scarcity and endures significant early struggles. The wealth promised by the yoga manifests only later in life, often after middle age.32
Chronologically, the Dasha sequence strictly dictates the exact timeline of these fluctuations. During the expansive Mahadasha of the Dhana Yoga lord, the native will experience significant accumulation. If the Antardasha of the Daridra Yoga lord arrives within that period, it will cause acute, localized financial setbacks or sudden debts; however, because the overarching Mahadasha is protective, these setbacks will not permanently override the prosperity, though they will test the native's resilience.27
Combustion (Asta) in Raja Yoga Formations
A highly debated topic in astrological research is whether the combustion (Asta) of a planet by the Sun nullifies its ability to form a Raja Yoga or Dhana Yoga.33 When a planet is positioned too close to the Sun, its physical rays are obscured by the solar glare, traditionally implying a severe loss of power and dignity.35
However, careful scrutiny of empirical data and chart analysis reveals that conjunction with the Sun does not deprive a planet of its Yoga-forming powers regarding macro-level worldly success and structural authority.33 If the ninth and tenth lords conjoin, creating a powerful Dharma-Karmadhipati Raja Yoga, but the tenth lord is combust, the native will still achieve the promised career elevation, public recognition, and leadership position.35 The yoga remains structurally sound.
The severe penalty of combustion is exacted instead upon the karakatwas (the natural, living significations) of the combust planet, rather than its house lordships.33 For example, if Saturn forms a Sasa Mahapurusha Yoga or a Raja Yoga but is deeply combust by the Sun, the native will undoubtedly achieve great professional status. However, because Saturn represents longevity, the nervous system, and subordinates, the native may suffer from chronic weakness in the limbs after age fifty, or endure a deeply strained, hostile relationship with employees and subordinates.35 Thus, the yoga works materially, granting the position of power, but severe physical and psychological stress is guaranteed. Extreme, exact combustion (within a single degree) can render the results highly unstable, making it exceedingly difficult for the native to maintain their position sustainably without burning out.35
Part 4: The Calculus of Multiple Yogas – Additive Versus Logarithmic Models
A prevalent misconception in modern astrological synthesis is the assumption of linear additivity—the belief that possessing three distinct Dhana Yogas makes an individual exactly three times wealthier than an individual possessing only one. The mechanical reality of chart analysis dictates that planetary combinations operate on a logarithmic model of diminishing returns, heavily influenced by the underlying balance of auspicious (Subha) and inauspicious (Asubha) forces within the specific horoscope.6
Subha to Asubha Ratios: The Logarithmic Nature of Chart Potency
Astrological evaluation algorithms calculate the net sum of positive and negative planetary judgments to determine the ultimate trajectory of a life.37 Advanced research into these scoring models reveals a profound truth about the baseline human condition: the average ratio of auspicious (Subha) to inauspicious (Asubha) values in a standard human chart is approximately 1:3.37
This inherent statistical asymmetry implies that negative yogas, afflictions, and karmic debts have a significantly greater downward gravitational pull than positive yogas have upward lift.37 The universe leans toward entropy and struggle. Consequently, the presence of the first powerful Raja Yoga or Dhana Yoga in a chart is monumental; it acts as a massive baseline corrective. This initial powerful yoga does the heavy lifting required to neutralize the inherent 1:3 negative bias of the human condition, pulling the native out of obscurity and into a realm of distinct success.6
If a second Dhana Yoga is present, it compounds this newly established success, pushing the native firmly into the upper echelons of wealth.6 However, the addition of a third or fourth Dhana Yoga yields noticeably diminishing returns. The energetic leap from poverty to substantial wealth (facilitated by Yoga 1) is exponentially larger in terms of measurable life impact than the leap from a millionaire to a multi-millionaire (facilitated by Yogas 3 and 4).6 Thus, the scoring is fundamentally logarithmic. The presence of interconnected, overlapping yogas guarantees an extraordinary destiny, but each subsequent yoga adds fractional, incremental value compared to the foundational combination, as environmental resistance to endless expansion increases.6
Advanced Metrics for Measuring Compounding Strength
To properly score and quantify the true strength of multiple yogas, advanced astrologers must utilize distinct mathematical systems rather than simple addition 14:
* Vaiseshikamsa Bala: This is the paramount metric for determining the sheer height, magnitude, and global reach that a Raja Yoga or Dhana Yoga can achieve.14 It measures a planet's dignity cumulatively across multiple divisional charts (Vargas), such as the D9 (Navamsa), D10 (Dasamsa), and D60 (Shashtiamsa). A planet forming a yoga in the D1 chart that also achieves high Vaiseshikamsa (for instance, residing in its own or exalted sign in six out of ten divisions) possesses profound compounding power. This is the metric that elevates a locally successful business owner to a figure of global, historical prominence.14
* Ashtakavarga System: While the Ashtakavarga system is not used to form yogas, the Sarvashtakavarga (SAV) and Bhinna Ashtakavarga (BAV) scores are essential for measuring the structural resilience and environmental support of the house where the yoga occurs.14 A Dhana Yoga forming in a house with a high SAV score (above thirty bindus) operates with frictionless environmental support; resources flow easily. Conversely, if a Dhana Yoga forms in a house with a low SAV score, the wealth will still manifest during the proper Dasha, but acquiring and retaining it will require immense, continuous, exhausting effort from the native.15
* Ishta and Kashta Phala: This mathematical ratio calculates the exact level of inherent auspiciousness a specific Dasha will yield.14 It determines if the planetary period will allow the multiple yogas to function smoothly, or if the period will be obstructed by internal friction and self-sabotage. Adding the points of the Mahadasha and Antardasha and dividing by two yields a score; if this number is below thirty, the results of the period, regardless of the yogas present, will lean toward the inauspicious and stressful.14
* Shadbala: Meaning "six strengths," this evaluates a planet based on its position, time, direction (Dikbala), motion, sight, and innate nature.39 A yoga formed by planets with exceptionally high Shadbala ensures that the native possesses the physical stamina, mental clarity, and directional focus to execute the yoga's promise flawlessly.14
